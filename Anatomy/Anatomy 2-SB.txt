Easy (Q1–35)
1. A 30-year-old man presents with severe right iliac fossa pain, nausea, and fever. On examination, there is tenderness at McBurney’s point. Which anatomical structure is most likely inflamed?
A) Cecum
B) Appendix
C) Terminal ileum
D) Right ovary
E) Sigmoid colon

Correct Answer: B
Explanation: McBurney’s point (1/3 from ASIS to umbilicus) overlies the base of the appendix. Tenderness here is classic for acute appendicitis.

2. A patient with a deep knife wound to the anterior neck at the cricoid cartilage level presents with aspiration of liquids. Which nerve is likely injured?
A) Recurrent laryngeal nerve
B) Superior laryngeal nerve (internal branch)
C) Superior laryngeal nerve (external branch)
D) Hypoglossal nerve
E) Glossopharyngeal nerve

Correct Answer: B
Explanation: The internal branch of the superior laryngeal nerve provides sensory innervation to the larynx above the vocal cords. Injury causes loss of cough reflex and aspiration.

3. A 50-year-old man with a right-sided stroke has difficulty swallowing. The physician suspects a lesion affecting the pharyngeal plexus. Which cranial nerve does NOT contribute to the pharyngeal plexus?
A) Glossopharyngeal (CN IX)
B) Vagus (CN X)
C) Spinal accessory (CN XI)
D) Hypoglossal (CN XII)
E) Sympathetic fibers

Correct Answer: D
Explanation: The pharyngeal plexus is formed by CN IX, CN X, CN XI (cranial root), and sympathetic fibers. CN XII (hypoglossal) is motor to the tongue, not part of the pharyngeal plexus.

4. A 25-year-old man sustains a blow to the lateral side of the knee. He now has a foot drop. Which nerve is most likely injured?
A) Tibial
B) Common peroneal
C) Sural
D) Saphenous
E) Superficial peroneal

Correct Answer: B
Explanation: The common peroneal (fibular) nerve winds around the fibular neck, making it vulnerable to trauma. Injury causes loss of dorsiflexion and foot drop.

5. A patient with severe chest pain after blunt trauma has a tension pneumothorax. Needle decompression is performed at the 2nd intercostal space, midclavicular line. What is the next layer encountered after the skin?
A) Pectoralis major
B) Intercostal muscle
C) External intercostal muscle
D) Pleura
E) Subcutaneous fat

Correct Answer: A
Explanation: At the 2nd ICS midclavicular line, the pectoralis major muscle lies immediately deep to the subcutaneous tissue and superficial fascia.

6. A 40-year-old woman with a breast lump undergoes a biopsy. The surgeon makes an incision in the upper outer quadrant. Which artery is most at risk for bleeding?
A) Internal thoracic
B) Lateral thoracic
C) Thoracoacromial
D) Intercostal
E) Subclavian

Correct Answer: B
Explanation: The lateral thoracic artery runs along the lateral border of the pectoralis minor and supplies the upper outer quadrant of the breast. It is commonly encountered during breast surgery.

7. A 60-year-old man with a known abdominal aortic aneurysm presents with sudden-onset scrotal pain. Which nerve carries referred pain from the aorta to the testis?
A) Ilioinguinal
B) Genitofemoral
C) Sympathetic fibers via the spermatic plexus
D) Obturator
E) Pudendal

Correct Answer: C
Explanation: Visceral afferents from the aorta travel with sympathetic fibers that accompany the testicular vessels, causing referred pain to the testicle (testicular pain).

8. A patient with a central line in the right subclavian vein develops a pneumothorax. Which anatomical structure is most likely punctured?
A) Lung apex
B) Subclavian artery
C) Brachiocephalic vein
D) Thoracic duct
E) Phrenic nerve

Correct Answer: A
Explanation: The lung apex extends above the first rib into the root of the neck. A misplaced subclavian needle can puncture the apical pleura, causing pneumothorax.

9. A 35-year-old man with a stab wound to the left 4th intercostal space at the sternal border develops cardiac tamponade. Which cardiac chamber is most likely injured?
A) Right atrium
B) Right ventricle
C) Left atrium
D) Left ventricle
E) Aorta

Correct Answer: B
Explanation: The right ventricle forms the majority of the anterior cardiac surface and lies directly behind the left 4th–5th intercostal spaces near the sternum.

10. A 70-year-old woman with a hip fracture undergoes surgery. Postoperatively, she cannot adduct her thigh. Which nerve is damaged?
A) Femoral
B) Obturator
C) Sciatic
D) Superior gluteal
E) Inferior gluteal

Correct Answer: B
Explanation: The obturator nerve (L2–L4) supplies the adductor muscles of the thigh. Injury (e.g., from pelvic surgery or retractor pressure) causes loss of hip adduction.

11. A patient with a thyroid mass undergoes a total thyroidectomy. Postoperatively, she has a weak, breathy voice. Which nerve is most likely injured?
A) Recurrent laryngeal
B) External branch of superior laryngeal
C) Internal branch of superior laryngeal
D) Hypoglossal
E) Phrenic

Correct Answer: A
Explanation: Unilateral recurrent laryngeal nerve injury causes ipsilateral vocal cord paralysis, leading to hoarseness and a weak, breathy voice.

12. A 45-year-old man with a gunshot wound to the epigastrium has a CT showing a laceration of the left lobe of the liver. Which ligament attaches the left lobe to the diaphragm?
A) Falciform
B) Coronary
C) Left triangular
D) Round
E) Hepatogastric

Correct Answer: C
Explanation: The left triangular ligament attaches the left lobe of the liver to the diaphragm. The right and left triangular ligaments are the lateral extensions of the coronary ligament.

13. A 30-year-old woman with a stab wound to the right lower chest at the 9th intercostal space, midaxillary line, has a right-sided hemothorax. Which artery is likely injured?
A) 9th intercostal artery
B) Internal thoracic artery
C) Musculophrenic artery
D) Superior epigastric artery
E) Lumbar artery

Correct Answer: A
Explanation: The 9th intercostal artery runs in the subcostal groove of the 9th rib. A stab at the 9th ICS will injure that intercostal artery, causing hemothorax.

14. A patient with a large hiatal hernia presents with reflux symptoms. Through which diaphragmatic hiatus does the hernia occur?
A) Aortic hiatus
B) Esophageal hiatus
C) Caval hiatus
D) Foramen of Morgagni
E) Foramen of Bochdalek

Correct Answer: B
Explanation: The esophageal hiatus (at T10 level) transmits the esophagus and vagal trunks. Hiatal hernias occur when the stomach protrudes through this hiatus into the thorax.

15. A 55-year-old man with chronic pancreatitis develops a pseudocyst in the lesser sac. Which structure forms the posterior wall of the lesser sac?
A) Stomach
B) Pancreas
C) Transverse mesocolon
D) Greater omentum
E) Left kidney

Correct Answer: B
Explanation: The lesser sac (omental bursa) is bounded posteriorly by the pancreas and left kidney, anteriorly by the stomach and lesser omentum.

16. A 25-year-old man with a fractured humeral shaft presents with wrist drop. Which nerve is injured?
A) Axillary
B) Radial
C) Median
D) Ulnar
E) Musculocutaneous

Correct Answer: B
Explanation: The radial nerve runs in the spiral groove of the humerus. Fracture of the humeral shaft can injure it, causing wrist drop (loss of wrist extensors).

17. A patient with a stab wound to the left upper quadrant presents with a pulsatile mass in the left upper abdomen. CT shows a splenic artery pseudoaneurysm. Which structure lies immediately posterior to the splenic artery?
A) Stomach
B) Pancreas
C) Left kidney
D) Left adrenal
E) Spleen

Correct Answer: B
Explanation: The splenic artery runs along the superior border of the pancreas. The body of the pancreas lies posterior to the splenic artery in its mid-course.

18. A 60-year-old woman with a large goiter presents with stridor and a visible mass in the neck. The recurrent laryngeal nerve is at risk during surgery. Where does the left recurrent laryngeal nerve loop around?
A) Subclavian artery
B) Aortic arch
C) Ligamentum arteriosum
D) Pulmonary trunk
E) Brachiocephalic artery

Correct Answer: B
Explanation: The left recurrent laryngeal nerve loops under the aortic arch, while the right recurrent laryngeal nerve loops under the right subclavian artery.

19. A 40-year-old man with blunt abdominal trauma has a seatbelt sign across the lower abdomen. Which spinal level is most commonly fractured in a Chance fracture?
A) T12
B) L1
C) L2
D) L3
E) L4

Correct Answer: B
Explanation: L1 is the most common level for Chance fractures (flexion-distraction injuries) due to the fulcrum of the seatbelt. Associated with bowel and mesenteric injuries.

20. A patient with a central line placed in the left internal jugular vein develops a chylothorax. Which structure is lacerated?
A) Right lymphatic duct
B) Thoracic duct
C) Azygos vein
D) Hemiazygos vein
E) Intercostal lymphatic

Correct Answer: B
Explanation: The thoracic duct ascends on the left side of the neck and empties into the left subclavian vein. Injury during left IJ cannulation can cause chyle leakage (chylothorax).

21. A 30-year-old woman with a deep cut to the palmar aspect of the wrist at the level of the flexor retinaculum presents with loss of thumb opposition. Which nerve is transected?
A) Median (recurrent branch)
B) Ulnar (deep branch)
C) Radial (superficial branch)
D) Median (palmar cutaneous)
E) Ulnar (dorsal cutaneous)

Correct Answer: A
Explanation: The recurrent (thenar) branch of the median nerve supplies the abductor pollicis brevis, opponens pollicis, and superficial head of flexor pollicis brevis. Injury causes loss of thumb opposition.

22. A patient with a right-sided pleural effusion undergoes thoracentesis at the 9th intercostal space, posterior axillary line. Which structure is at risk if the needle is inserted too low?
A) Liver
B) Spleen
C) Kidney
D) Diaphragm
E) Lung

Correct Answer: A
Explanation: The right hemidiaphragm dome is at the 5th ICS anteriorly, but posteriorly the 9th ICS is below the lung but above the liver. A needle inserted too low (below 9th ICS) can puncture the liver.

23. A 65-year-old man with a history of myocardial infarction presents with acute shortness of breath. He is found to have a ruptured papillary muscle. Which papillary muscle is more commonly ruptured after an inferior wall MI?
A) Anterolateral
B) Posteromedial
C) Anterior (right ventricle)
D) Posterior (right ventricle)
E) Septal

Correct Answer: B
Explanation: The posteromedial papillary muscle has a single blood supply from the posterior descending artery (usually right coronary), making it more vulnerable to infarction and rupture.

24. A 50-year-old woman with a right-sided breast cancer undergoes a modified radical mastectomy. Which nerve is most at risk during axillary dissection?
A) Long thoracic
B) Thoracodorsal
C) Intercostobrachial
D) Medial pectoral
E) Lateral pectoral

Correct Answer: C
Explanation: The intercostobrachial nerve (T2) provides sensation to the medial arm and axilla. It is commonly injured during axillary dissection, causing numbness of the arm.

25. A 20-year-old man with a stab wound to the left anterior chest at the 5th intercostal space, midclavicular line, develops hypotension. Which structure is injured?
A) Left ventricle
B) Right ventricle

E) Aorta

Correct Answer: B
Explanation: The right ventricle forms the anterior cardiac surface and lies behind the 4th–6th intercostal spaces. A stab at the 5th ICS midclavicular line will penetrate the right ventricle.

26. A patient with a large left-sided pneumothorax has a chest tube inserted in the 4th intercostal space, anterior axillary line. Which muscle is traversed first after the skin?
A) Pectoralis major
B) Serratus anterior
C) Latissimus dorsi
D) External intercostal
E) Internal intercostal

Correct Answer: B
Explanation: At the anterior axillary line, the serratus anterior is the most superficial muscle overlying the ribs. The chest tube passes through it before reaching the intercostal muscles.

27. A 55-year-old man with a left-sided hydrocele undergoes aspiration. The needle is inserted through the scrotal skin. Which layer is the last before entering the tunica vaginalis?
A) External spermatic fascia
B) Cremasteric fascia

D) Dartos fascia
E) Tunica albuginea

Correct Answer: C
Explanation: The scrotal layers: skin, dartos, external spermatic fascia, cremasteric fascia, internal spermatic fascia, then tunica vaginalis. The internal spermatic fascia is the last fascial layer before the vaginal sac.

28. A 40-year-old woman with a stab wound to the right upper quadrant at the 7th intercostal space, midclavicular line, has a liver laceration. Which segment of the liver is most likely injured?
A) Segment II
B) Segment III
C) Segment IV
D) Segment V

Correct Answer: D
Explanation: Segment V (right anterior inferior) lies just deep to the 6th–7th intercostal spaces anteriorly. A stab at the 7th ICS midclavicular line typically injures segment V or VI.

29. A patient with a thyroid nodule undergoes a fine-needle aspiration. The needle passes through the platysma and the investing layer of deep cervical fascia. Which muscle lies immediately deep to the investing fascia in the anterior triangle?
A) Sternocleidomastoid
B) Sternohyoid
C) Omohyoid

E) Scalene

Correct Answer: B
Explanation: In the anterior midline, the investing fascia covers the sternohyoid muscle (the most superficial of the infrahyoid strap muscles). The sternocleidomastoid is lateral.

30. A 60-year-old man with a history of alcohol abuse presents with hematemesis. Endoscopy shows esophageal varices. Which portosystemic anastomosis is responsible?
A) Left gastric to azygos
B) Superior mesenteric to inferior vena cava
C) Splenic to left renal
D) Umbilical to epigastric
E) Rectal to internal iliac

Correct Answer: A
Explanation: The left gastric (coronary) vein drains into the portal system and anastomoses with esophageal veins (azygos system). Portal hypertension causes esophageal varices at this site.

A) Lingual
B) Inferior alveolar
C) Mental
D) Buccal
E) Auriculotemporal

Explanation: The inferior alveolar nerve (branch of mandibular V3) runs through the mandibular canal. Fracture can injure it, causing lower lip anesthesia (the mental nerve is its terminal branch).

32. A 70-year-old woman with a hip fracture undergoes a hemiarthroplasty. During the posterior approach, which nerve is most at risk?
A) Femoral
B) Obturator
C) Sciatic
D) Superior gluteal
E) Inferior gluteal

B) Internal thoracic

34. A 45-year-old man with a stab wound to the left flank at the 12th rib angle has a CT showing a perinephric hematoma. Which part of the kidney is most vulnerable?

Explanation: The lower pole of the kidney is less protected by ribs (the 12th rib ends at the upper pole). A stab below the 12th rib easily injures the lower pole.

Medium (Q36–70)
36. A 50-year-old man with a large right-sided pleural effusion undergoes thoracentesis. The needle is inserted just above the 8th rib in the posterior axillary line. Which anatomical principle prevents injury to the neurovascular bundle?
A) The bundle runs above the rib
B) The bundle runs in the subcostal groove, below the rib
C) The bundle is protected by the pleura
D) The bundle is midline
E) The bundle is extrapleural

Correct Answer: B
Explanation: The intercostal neurovascular bundle runs in the subcostal groove of the upper rib. Needle insertion just above the lower rib avoids the bundle of the rib below.

37. A patient with a large goiter undergoes thyroidectomy. Postoperatively, he has stridor and respiratory distress. Which nerve injury is most likely?
A) Unilateral recurrent laryngeal
B) Bilateral recurrent laryngeal
C) External branch of superior laryngeal
D) Internal branch of superior laryngeal
E) Phrenic

Correct Answer: B
Explanation: Bilateral recurrent laryngeal nerve injury causes both vocal cords to assume a paramedian position, leading to airway obstruction (stridor) and aphonia.

38. A 60-year-old man with a ruptured abdominal aortic aneurysm undergoes emergency repair. The surgeon cross-clamps the aorta just below the renal arteries. Which collateral pathway can maintain spinal cord perfusion?
A) Artery of Adamkiewicz from a lumbar artery
B) Internal thoracic to intercostal arteries
C) Vertebral to posterior intercostal
D) Superior mesenteric to lumbar
E) Iliolumbar to anterior spinal

Correct Answer: A
Explanation: The artery of Adamkiewicz (great radicular artery) arises from a left posterior intercostal or lumbar artery (T9–L2) and supplies the lower spinal cord. It is a critical collateral after aortic clamping.

39. A 35-year-old woman with a stab wound to the left posterior triangle of the neck presents with a winged scapula. Which nerve is injured?
A) Long thoracic (C5–C7)
B) Spinal accessory (CN XI)
C) Dorsal scapular
D) Thoracodorsal
E) Suprascapular

Explanation: The long thoracic nerve runs superficially on the serratus anterior muscle, passing through the posterior triangle. Injury causes medial winging of the scapula.

A) Common hepatic
B) Proper hepatic
C) Left gastric

Explanation: The gastroduodenal artery is the first branch of the common hepatic artery (from the celiac trunk). It passes posterior to the first part of the duodenum.
