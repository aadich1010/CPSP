Easy (Q1–35)
1. A 55-year-old man with severe epigastric pain radiating to the back is diagnosed with acute pancreatitis. Which anatomical structure lies posterior to the pancreas and can be involved in retroperitoneal spread of inflammation?
A) Left kidney
B) Abdominal aorta
C) Inferior vena cava
D) Right kidney
E) Spleen

Correct Answer: B
Explanation: The abdominal aorta lies directly posterior to the body of the pancreas. Severe pancreatitis can cause retroperitoneal inflammation involving the aorta and surrounding structures.

2. A 30-year-old man with a stab wound to the right 5th intercostal space at the midclavicular line presents with hypotension and distended neck veins. Which cardiac chamber is most likely injured?
A) Right atrium
B) Right ventricle
C) Left atrium
D) Left ventricle
E) Pulmonary trunk

Correct Answer: B
Explanation: The right ventricle forms most of the anterior cardiac surface and lies behind the 4th–6th intercostal spaces. A stab at the 5th ICS midclavicular line often penetrates the right ventricle, causing tamponade.

3. A 65-year-old man with a left apical lung mass presents with hoarseness. Which nerve is compressed as it loops under the aortic arch?
A) Left recurrent laryngeal
B) Right recurrent laryngeal
C) Left vagus
D) Right phrenic
E) Left phrenic

Correct Answer: A
Explanation: The left recurrent laryngeal nerve branches from the vagus and loops under the aortic arch. A left apical lung mass can compress it, causing hoarseness due to vocal cord paralysis.

4. A patient with cirrhosis develops massive ascites. Paracentesis is planned. Which anatomical landmark is safest to avoid injury to the inferior epigastric artery?
A) Midline below the umbilicus
B) Left lower quadrant 2 cm medial to ASIS
C) Right upper quadrant
D) Suprapubic region
E) Flank area

Correct Answer: A
Explanation: The inferior epigastric artery runs lateral to the rectus abdominis. Midline puncture (linea alba) is avascular and safest for paracentesis to avoid vessel injury.

5. A 25-year-old man with a fractured humeral shaft cannot extend his wrist or fingers. Which nerve is most likely injured?
A) Axillary
B) Radial
C) Median
D) Ulnar
E) Musculocutaneous

Correct Answer: B
Explanation: The radial nerve runs in the spiral groove of the humerus. Fracture of the humeral shaft often injures it, causing wrist drop (loss of wrist and finger extensors).

6. A 40-year-old woman with a thyroid nodule undergoes fine-needle aspiration. The needle passes through the platysma. What is the next fascial layer encountered?
A) Superficial cervical fascia
B) Investing layer of deep cervical fascia
C) Pretracheal fascia
D) Prevertebral fascia
E) Carotid sheath

Correct Answer: B
Explanation: After skin and platysma (in superficial fascia), the next layer is the investing layer of deep cervical fascia, which encloses the sternocleidomastoid and trapezius muscles.

7. A patient with a large right-sided pleural effusion undergoes thoracentesis. The needle is inserted at the 9th intercostal space, midaxillary line. Insertion too low may puncture which organ?
A) Liver
B) Spleen
C) Kidney
D) Lung
E) Diaphragm

Correct Answer: A
Explanation: The right hemidiaphragm dome is at the 5th ICS anteriorly, but posteriorly the 9th ICS is above the liver. However, a needle inserted below the 9th ICS risks puncturing the liver.

8. A 60-year-old man with benign prostatic hyperplasia has acute urinary retention. A suprapubic catheter is placed. Which structure lies immediately posterior to the bladder?
A) Rectum
B) Prostate
C) Ureter
D) Seminal vesicle
E) Iliac vessels

Correct Answer: A
Explanation: The rectum lies directly posterior to the bladder, separated only by Denonvilliers’ fascia. Suprapubic catheter placement, if too deep, can perforate the rectum.

9. A 35-year-old man with a stab wound to the left upper quadrant has a splenic injury. Which artery is the main blood supply to the spleen and is at risk during splenectomy?
A) Left gastric
B) Short gastric
C) Splenic
D) Left gastroepiploic
E) Inferior phrenic

Correct Answer: C
Explanation: The splenic artery (branch of celiac trunk) is the main arterial supply to the spleen. It runs along the superior border of the pancreas and must be ligated during splenectomy.

10. A 55-year-old woman with a central venous catheter in the right internal jugular vein develops a pneumothorax. Which structure is most likely punctured?
A) Subclavian artery
B) Lung apex
C) Thoracic duct
D) Phrenic nerve
E) Brachiocephalic vein

Correct Answer: B
Explanation: The lung apex (cupula) extends above the first rib into the root of the neck, posterior to the internal jugular vein. Deep needle insertion can puncture the pleura, causing pneumothorax.

11. A patient with a deep cut to the medial aspect of the mid-thigh cannot adduct the thigh. Which nerve is injured?
A) Femoral
B) Obturator
C) Sciatic
D) Saphenous
E) Lateral femoral cutaneous

Correct Answer: B
Explanation: The obturator nerve (L2–L4) supplies the adductor muscles of the thigh. Injury from a medial thigh wound causes loss of hip adduction.

12. A 70-year-old man with a left-sided inguinal hernia undergoes repair. The hernia sac is lateral to the inferior epigastric vessels. This is an indirect hernia. Which structure does the sac follow?
A) Femoral canal
B) Deep inguinal ring
C) Superficial inguinal ring
D) Hesselbach’s triangle
E) Obturator foramen

Correct Answer: B
Explanation: Indirect inguinal hernias pass through the deep inguinal ring (lateral to inferior epigastric vessels) and follow the spermatic cord into the scrotum.

13. A 45-year-old man with a knife wound to the anterior neck at the cricoid cartilage level presents with stridor and hoarseness. Which structure is transected?
A) Trachea
B) Esophagus
C) Recurrent laryngeal nerve
D) Common carotid
E) Internal jugular

Correct Answer: C
Explanation: The recurrent laryngeal nerve runs in the tracheoesophageal groove. A wound at cricoid level (C6) can damage the nerve, causing ipsilateral vocal cord paralysis and stridor.

14. A patient with a large goiter presents with dysphagia. Which structure is compressed by the enlarged thyroid?
A) Trachea
B) Esophagus
C) Recurrent laryngeal nerve
D) Common carotid
E) Internal jugular

Correct Answer: B
Explanation: The esophagus lies posterior to the trachea and left lobe of the thyroid. A large goiter can compress the esophagus, causing dysphagia (difficulty swallowing).

15. A 30-year-old woman with a stab wound to the right 8th intercostal space, midaxillary line, has a right-sided hemothorax. Which vessel is most likely injured?
A) 8th intercostal artery
B) Internal thoracic artery
C) Musculophrenic artery
D) Superior epigastric artery
E) Lateral thoracic artery

Correct Answer: A
Explanation: The 8th intercostal artery runs in the subcostal groove of the 8th rib. A stab at the 8th ICS will injure this artery, causing bleeding into the pleural space.

16. A 50-year-old man with a history of alcohol abuse presents with hematemesis. Endoscopy shows esophageal varices. Which vein carries portal blood to the azygos system?
A) Left gastric
B) Splenic
C) Superior mesenteric
D) Inferior mesenteric
E) Paraumbilical

Correct Answer: A
Explanation: The left gastric (coronary) vein drains the portal system and anastomoses with esophageal veins (azygos system). Portal hypertension leads to esophageal varices at this site.

17. A 20-year-old man with a fractured mandible has numbness of the lower lip and chin. Which nerve is injured?
A) Lingual
B) Inferior alveolar
C) Mental
D) Buccal
E) Auriculotemporal

Correct Answer: B
Explanation: The inferior alveolar nerve (branch of mandibular V3) runs through the mandibular canal. Fracture can injure it; the mental nerve is its terminal branch supplying the lower lip and chin.

18. A patient with a large left-sided pneumothorax has a chest tube inserted at the 5th intercostal space, anterior axillary line. Which muscle is first encountered after skin?
A) Pectoralis major
B) Serratus anterior
C) Latissimus dorsi
D) External intercostal
E) Internal intercostal

Correct Answer: B
Explanation: At the anterior axillary line, the serratus anterior is the most superficial muscle overlying the ribs. The chest tube passes through it before reaching the intercostals.

19. A 65-year-old woman with a femoral neck fracture undergoes hip replacement. During the posterior approach, which nerve is most at risk?
A) Sciatic
B) Femoral
C) Obturator
D) Superior gluteal
E) Inferior gluteal

Correct Answer: A
Explanation: The sciatic nerve lies posterior to the hip joint and is vulnerable during posterior hip approaches. Retractor placement can injure it, causing foot drop or leg weakness.

20. A 40-year-old man with a stab wound to the epigastrium has a CT showing a pancreatic injury. Which part of the pancreas is most commonly injured in penetrating trauma?
A) Head
B) Neck
C) Body
D) Tail
E) Uncinate process

Correct Answer: C
Explanation: The body of the pancreas is the most anterior and least protected part, lying directly posterior to the stomach. It is most commonly injured in penetrating epigastric trauma.

21. A patient with a right-sided stroke has difficulty swallowing (dysphagia). Which cranial nerve is most likely affected?
A) Trigeminal (CN V)
B) Facial (CN VII)
C) Glossopharyngeal (CN IX)
D) Vagus (CN X)
E) Hypoglossal (CN XII)

Correct Answer: D
Explanation: The vagus nerve (CN X) provides motor innervation to the pharyngeal constrictors and laryngeal muscles. Lesions cause dysphagia and dysphonia.

22. A 55-year-old man with a history of smoking presents with a right apical lung mass and right-sided Horner’s syndrome (ptosis, miosis, anhidrosis). Which structure is invaded?
A) Stellate ganglion
B) Phrenic nerve
C) Vagus nerve
D) Recurrent laryngeal nerve
E) Brachial plexus

Correct Answer: A
Explanation: The stellate ganglion (cervicothoracic sympathetic ganglion) lies at the apex of the lung. A Pancoast tumor invades it, causing ipsilateral Horner’s syndrome.

23. A 30-year-old woman with a deep cut to the volar forearm, 2 cm proximal to the wrist, has loss of thumb opposition. Which nerve is cut?
A) Median (recurrent branch)
B) Ulnar (deep branch)
C) Radial (superficial)
D) Median (palmar cutaneous)
E) Ulnar (dorsal cutaneous)

Correct Answer: A
Explanation: The recurrent (thenar) branch of the median nerve supplies the thenar muscles (opponens pollicis, abductor pollicis brevis). Injury at the wrist causes loss of thumb opposition.

24. A patient with a large hydrocele undergoes aspiration. The needle passes through the scrotal skin. Which layer is the last before entering the tunica vaginalis?
A) External spermatic fascia
B) Cremasteric fascia
C) Internal spermatic fascia
D) Dartos fascia
E) Tunica albuginea

Correct Answer: C
Explanation: The scrotal layers from superficial to deep: skin, dartos, external spermatic fascia, cremasteric fascia, internal spermatic fascia, then tunica vaginalis. Internal spermatic fascia is the last fascial layer.

25. A 60-year-old man with a left ventricular aneurysm develops acute mitral regurgitation. Which papillary muscle is most commonly ruptured after an inferior wall myocardial infarction?
A) Anterolateral
B) Posteromedial
C) Anterior (right ventricle)
D) Posterior (right ventricle)
E) Septal

Correct Answer: B
Explanation: The posteromedial papillary muscle has a single blood supply (posterior descending artery, usually from RCA). Inferior MI can cause its rupture, leading to acute mitral regurgitation.

26. A 45-year-old woman with a thyroid mass undergoes a total thyroidectomy. Postoperatively, she has a weak, breathy voice. Which nerve is most likely injured?
A) Recurrent laryngeal
B) External branch of superior laryngeal
C) Internal branch of superior laryngeal
D) Hypoglossal
E) Spinal accessory

Correct Answer: A
Explanation: Unilateral recurrent laryngeal nerve injury causes ipsilateral vocal cord paralysis, resulting in hoarseness and a weak, breathy voice. This is a common complication of thyroid surgery.

27. A patient with a stab wound to the left lower quadrant presents with a pulsatile mass in the left groin. Which artery is injured?
A) Common femoral
B) External iliac
C) Internal iliac
D) Superficial femoral
E) Deep femoral

Correct Answer: B
Explanation: The external iliac artery passes along the pelvic brim. A stab wound to the lower quadrant can injure it, leading to a pseudoaneurysm presenting as a pulsatile groin mass.

28. A 70-year-old man with a known abdominal aortic aneurysm presents with sudden-onset testicular pain. Which nerve carries referred pain from the aorta?
A) Ilioinguinal
B) Genitofemoral
C) Sympathetic fibers via spermatic plexus
D) Obturator
E) Pudendal

Correct Answer: C
Explanation: Visceral afferent fibers from the aorta travel with sympathetic nerves that follow the testicular vessels, causing referred pain to the testicle (a classic sign of leaking AAA).

29. A 35-year-old woman with a deep cut to the palmar aspect of the hand at the base of the thumb has loss of sensation over the palmar aspect of the index finger. Which nerve is injured?
A) Median
B) Ulnar
C) Radial
D) Palmar cutaneous branch of median
E) Superficial radial

Correct Answer: A
Explanation: The median nerve provides sensation to the palmar aspect of the lateral 3½ fingers (including the index finger). Injury at the wrist causes sensory loss in that distribution.

30. A patient with a central line placed in the left subclavian vein develops a chylothorax. Which duct is lacerated?
A) Right lymphatic duct
B) Thoracic duct
C) Azygos vein
D) Hemiazygos vein
E) Intercostal lymphatic

Correct Answer: B
Explanation: The thoracic duct empties into the left subclavian vein. Puncture during left subclavian cannulation can lacerate it, causing chylothorax (milky pleural effusion).

31. A 50-year-old man with a right-sided pneumothorax has a chest tube inserted at the 4th intercostal space, midaxillary line. Which nerve runs vertically on the serratus anterior and is at risk?
A) Long thoracic
B) Thoracodorsal
C) Intercostobrachial
D) Phrenic
E) Vagus

Correct Answer: A
Explanation: The long thoracic nerve (C5–C7) runs vertically on the surface of the serratus anterior muscle. A chest tube inserted too far laterally can injure it, causing winged scapula.

32. A 60-year-old woman with a large goiter presents with stridor. Which structure is compressed causing airway obstruction?
A) Esophagus
B) Trachea
C) Recurrent laryngeal nerve
D) Phrenic nerve
E) Carotid artery

Correct Answer: B
Explanation: A large goiter can compress the trachea, causing stridor (noisy breathing) and respiratory distress. This is a surgical emergency.

33. A 25-year-old man with a fractured pelvis after a motor vehicle accident cannot flex his hip. Which nerve is most likely injured?
A) Femoral
B) Obturator
C) Sciatic
D) Superior gluteal
E) Inferior gluteal

Correct Answer: A
Explanation: The femoral nerve (L2–L4) supplies the iliopsoas (hip flexor) and quadriceps. Pelvic fractures can injure it, causing loss of hip flexion and knee extension.

34. A patient with a stab wound to the right 10th intercostal space, posterior axillary line, has a CT showing a liver laceration. Which ligament attaches the liver to the diaphragm and is torn?
A) Falciform
B) Coronary
C) Left triangular
D) Round
E) Hepatogastric

Correct Answer: B
Explanation: The coronary ligament attaches the liver to the diaphragm. A penetrating injury at the 10th ICS posteriorly is near the bare area of the liver, tearing the coronary ligament.

35. A 40-year-old man with chronic pancreatitis develops a pseudocyst. The pseudocyst is located in the lesser sac. Which structure forms the anterior wall of the lesser sac?
A) Stomach
B) Lesser omentum
C) Greater omentum
D) Transverse mesocolon

Correct Answer: B
Explanation: The lesser omentum (hepatogastric and hepatoduodenal ligaments) forms the anterior boundary of the lesser sac (omental bursa). The stomach is more anterior.

Medium (Q36–70)
36. A 55-year-old man with a right-sided lung cancer undergoes a lobectomy. During surgery, the surgeon must identify the right recurrent laryngeal nerve. Where does it loop?
A) Aortic arch
B) Right subclavian artery
C) Brachiocephalic artery
D) Pulmonary trunk
E) Ligamentum arteriosum

Correct Answer: B
Explanation: The right recurrent laryngeal nerve branches from the vagus and loops under the right subclavian artery. The left recurrent laryngeal loops under the aortic arch.

37. A 30-year-old woman with a stab wound to the left posterior triangle of the neck presents with a winged scapula (medial winging). Which nerve is injured?
A) Long thoracic
B) Spinal accessory
C) Dorsal scapular
D) Thoracodorsal
E) Suprascapular

Correct Answer: A
Explanation: The long thoracic nerve runs on the serratus anterior. Injury causes medial winging of the scapula (the medial border lifts off the chest wall when pushing against a wall).

38. A 65-year-old man with a ruptured abdominal aortic aneurysm undergoes emergency repair. The surgeon cross-clamps the aorta above the renal arteries. Which collateral pathway maintains lower limb perfusion?
A) Internal thoracic to inferior epigastric
B) Superior mesenteric to inferior mesenteric (Riolan’s arch)
C) Lumbar to iliolumbar
D) Vertebral to intercostal
E) Gonadal to circumflex iliac

Correct Answer: B
Explanation: The marginal artery of Drummond (or Riolan’s arch) connects the superior mesenteric and inferior mesenteric arteries, providing collateral flow to the lower limbs when the aorta is clamped above the renals.

39. A 45-year-old man with a pancreatic head tumor presents with painless jaundice, a palpable gallbladder (Courvoisier’s sign), and weight loss. The tumor is compressing which structure?
A) Common bile duct
B) Main pancreatic duct
C) Portal vein
D) Superior mesenteric artery
E) Hepatic artery

Correct Answer: A
Explanation: The common bile duct passes through the head of the pancreas. A pancreatic head tumor compresses the CBD, causing obstructive jaundice. The gallbladder becomes palpable due to distention.

40. A 50-year-old woman with a large right ovarian cyst undergoes oophorectomy. During surgery, the ureter is identified crossing which artery at the pelvic brim?
A) Common iliac
B) External iliac
C) Internal iliac
D) Ovarian
E) Uterine

Correct Answer: B
Explanation: The ureter crosses the external iliac artery at the pelvic brim, just medial to the ovarian vessels. This is a key landmark to avoid ureteral injury during pelvic surgery.

41. A patient with a stab wound to the left 5th intercostal space, midclavicular line, develops a hemopericardium. Which cardiac chamber is most likely injured?
A) Right atrium
B) Right ventricle
C) Left atrium

42. A 60-year-old man with a history of smoking presents with a Pancoast tumor. Which structures are involved in Pancoast syndrome?
A) Lower trunk of brachial plexus (C8–T1) and stellate ganglion
B) Upper trunk of brachial plexus (C5–C6) and phrenic nerve
C) Middle trunk and recurrent laryngeal nerve
D) Sympathetic chain alone
E) Subclavian artery and vein

Correct Answer: A
Explanation: Pancoast syndrome includes shoulder pain (C8–T1), hand weakness (lower trunk), and Horner’s (stellate ganglion). The subclavian artery may be involved but is not part of the classic triad.

43. A 35-year-old man with a deep cut to the volar forearm, 8 cm proximal to the wrist, has loss of flexion of the distal interphalangeal joint of the index finger. Which nerve is injured?

E) Ulnar (deep)

Correct Answer: A
Explanation: The anterior interosseous nerve (branch of median) supplies flexor digitorum profundus to index and middle fingers. Injury causes loss of index DIP flexion (positive “OK” sign).

44. A patient with a large left-sided pleural effusion undergoes thoracentesis. The needle is inserted at the 8th intercostal space, posterior axillary line, just above the 8th rib. Which structure is immediately deep?
A) Lung
B) Diaphragm
C) Spleen
D) Kidney
E) Liver

Correct Answer: A
Explanation: At the 8th ICS posterior axillary line, the lung extends to the 10th rib posteriorly. The needle enters the pleural space and lung, not the diaphragm (which is lower posteriorly).

45. A 70-year-old man with a large inguinal hernia undergoes repair. The hernia sac is found medial to the inferior epigastric vessels. This is a direct hernia. Which triangle is defined by these vessels?
A) Hesselbach’s triangle
B) Scarp’s triangle
C) Petit’s triangle
D) Grynfelt’s triangle
E) Calot’s triangle

Correct Answer: A
Explanation: Hesselbach’s triangle is bounded by the inferior epigastric vessels (laterally), rectus abdominis (medially), and inguinal ligament (inferiorly). Direct hernias occur within this triangle.

46. A 40-year-old woman with a central line in the right internal jugular vein develops a right-sided Horner’s syndrome. Which structure was injured?

Explanation: The stellate ganglion (cervicothoracic sympathetic ganglion) lies on the longus colli muscle at C7. A deep IJ puncture can injure it, causing ipsilateral Horner’s syndrome.

47. A 55-year-old man with a retroperitoneal sarcoma undergoes resection. The tumor is located anterior to the psoas muscle, medial to the ureter, and lateral to the aorta. Which lymph node group is involved?
A) Para-aortic
B) Paracaval
C) Iliac
D) Mesenteric
E) Retrocrural

Correct Answer: A
Explanation: Para-aortic lymph nodes lie along the abdominal aorta, anterior to the psoas, medial to ureters. This location is classic for retroperitoneal sarcomas or lymphoma.

Explanation: The gastrosplenic ligament connects the stomach to the spleen and contains the short gastric arteries. During splenectomy, this ligament is ligated to avoid gastric ischemia.

A) Common femoral
B) Superficial femoral
C) Deep femoral

A) External spermatic fascia

Explanation: The scrotal layers from superficial to deep: skin, dartos fascia, external spermatic fascia (from external oblique aponeurosis), cremasteric fascia, internal spermatic fascia, then tunica vaginalis.

A) Recurrent laryngeal
B) External branch of superior laryngeal
C) Internal branch of superior laryngeal
D) Hypoglossal
E) Spinal accessory

Correct Answer: B
Explanation: The external branch of the superior laryngeal nerve supplies the cricothyroid muscle, which tenses the vocal cord. Injury causes loss of high pitch, voice fatigue, and mild hoarseness.

53. A 60-year-old man with a history of COPD presents with a spontaneous pneumothorax. A chest tube is placed in the 4th intercostal space, anterior axillary line. Which vessel runs in the subcostal groove of the 4th rib and is at risk?
A) Intercostal artery
B) Internal thoracic artery
C) Lateral thoracic artery
D) Thoracoacromial artery
E) Superior epigastric artery

Correct Answer: A
Explanation: The intercostal artery, vein, and nerve run in the subcostal groove of each rib. A chest tube placed too low (below the rib) can injure the neurovascular bundle.

54. A 35-year-old woman with a stab wound to the right upper quadrant at the 6th intercostal space, anterior axillary line, has a liver laceration. Which segment of the liver is most likely injured?
A) Segment II
B) Segment III
C) Segment IV
D) Segment V
E) Segment VII

Explanation: The right lobe anterior inferior segment (Segment V) lies just deep to the 6th–7th intercostal spaces anteriorly. A stab here commonly injures Segment V or VI.

55. A patient with a central line placed in the right internal jugular vein develops a hemothorax. The needle likely injured which artery?
A) Carotid
B) Subclavian
C) Vertebral
D) Thyrocervical trunk
E) Internal thoracic

Correct Answer: B
Explanation: The subclavian artery lies posterior to the clavicle and medial to the internal jugular vein. A low IJ approach angled too laterally can puncture the subclavian artery, bleeding into the pleural space.

56. A 45-year-old man with a gunshot wound to the epigastrium has a CT showing injury to the celiac trunk. Which organs would be most immediately ischemic?
A) Liver, spleen, stomach
B) Kidneys, adrenals
C) Small intestine, cecum
D) Descending colon
E) Pancreas, duodenum

Correct Answer: A
Explanation: The celiac trunk supplies the foregut: liver (via hepatic artery), spleen (via splenic artery), and stomach (via left gastric artery). The pancreas and duodenum receive additional supply from SMA.

57. A 70-year-old woman with a large hiatal hernia presents with chest pain and dysphagia. Which anatomical hiatus is involved, and what structure passes through it?

C) Caval hiatus – IVC and phrenic nerve
D) Foramen of Morgagni – internal thoracic vessels

Correct Answer: B
Explanation: The esophageal hiatus is in the right crus of the diaphragm at T10, passing esophagus and anterior/posterior vagal trunks. A hiatal hernia occurs when stomach herniates through this hiatus.

58. A patient with a pancreatic pseudocyst presents with sudden-onset left upper quadrant pain and a palpable mass. The pseudocyst has ruptured into which space?
A) Lesser sac
B) Greater sac
C) Retroperitoneum
D) Left subphrenic space
E) Left paracolic gutter

Correct Answer: A
Explanation: Pancreatic pseudocysts typically form in the lesser sac (omental bursa), which lies posterior to the stomach. Rupture often remains localized but can extend into the greater sac.

59. A 25-year-old man with a stab wound to the left anterior chest at the 2nd intercostal space, parasternal region, develops a large left pneumothorax. Which anatomical space is entered?

B) Pericardial cavity

D) Peritoneal cavity
E) Subcutaneous tissue

Explanation: The 2nd intercostal space parasternal region overlies the left upper lobe and pleural cavity. Air enters the pleural space, causing pneumothorax. The heart is deeper and more medial.

60. A 60-year-old man with a pseudoaneurysm of the superior mesenteric artery (SMA). Which structure passes anterior to the SMA at its origin from the aorta?
A) Left renal vein
B) Uncinate process of pancreas

E) Portal vein

Explanation: The uncinate process of the pancreas lies anterior to the SMA origin. The left renal vein passes between aorta and SMA (posterior to SMA). The third part of duodenum passes posterior to SMA.

A) L2–L4 between psoas and iliacus
B) L1–L3 between psoas and quadratus lumborum
