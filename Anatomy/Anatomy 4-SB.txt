Easy (Q1–35)
1. A 45-year-old man presents with sudden-onset severe chest pain that is worse when lying flat and improves by leaning forward. He has a pericardial friction rub. Which layer of the pericardium is inflamed?
A) Fibrous pericardium
B) Serous parietal pericardium
C) Serous visceral pericardium (epicardium)
D) Mediastinal pleura
E) Endocardium

Correct Answer: B
Explanation: Acute pericarditis involves the serous parietal pericardium, which lines the inner surface of the fibrous pericardium. The friction rub is due to inflamed layers rubbing against each other.

2. A 60-year-old man with a history of hypertension presents with a tearing sensation in the chest radiating to the back. A CT shows aortic dissection. Which layer of the aortic wall is most commonly the site of the intimal tear?
A) Tunica intima
B) Tunica media
C) Tunica adventitia
D) Internal elastic lamina
E) External elastic lamina

Correct Answer: A
Explanation: Aortic dissection begins with an intimal tear, allowing blood to enter the media and create a false lumen. The intima is the innermost layer.

3. A 30-year-old woman with a stab wound to the right upper quadrant has a liver laceration. Which peritoneal ligament attaches the liver to the anterior abdominal wall and contains the round ligament?
A) Falciform ligament
B) Coronary ligament
C) Left triangular ligament
D) Right triangular ligament
E) Hepatogastric ligament

Correct Answer: A
Explanation: The falciform ligament attaches the liver to the anterior abdominal wall and diaphragm. It contains the ligamentum teres (round ligament of the liver), a remnant of the umbilical vein.

4. A 55-year-old man with a large left-sided pleural effusion undergoes thoracentesis. The needle is inserted in the 9th intercostal space, midaxillary line. Which muscle is the first to be traversed after the skin?
A) Latissimus dorsi
B) Serratus anterior
C) External oblique
D) Pectoralis major
E) Trapezius

Correct Answer: B
Explanation: At the midaxillary line, the serratus anterior muscle overlies the ribs. It is the first muscle encountered after skin and subcutaneous tissue in this location.

5. A 25-year-old man with a fractured clavicle presents with numbness over the lateral aspect of the shoulder. Which nerve is most likely injured?
A) Axillary
B) Supraclavicular
C) Suprascapular
D) Long thoracic
E) Dorsal scapular

Correct Answer: B
Explanation: The supraclavicular nerves (C3–C4) provide sensation to the skin over the shoulder and upper chest. A fractured clavicle can injure these branches, causing lateral shoulder numbness.

6. A patient with a large goiter presents with difficulty breathing when lying flat. Which structure is compressed by the enlarged thyroid?
A) Esophagus
B) Trachea
C) Recurrent laryngeal nerve
D) Carotid artery
E) Jugular vein

Correct Answer: B
Explanation: A large goiter can compress the trachea, especially in the supine position, causing stridor and dyspnea. This is a surgical emergency if severe.

7. A 40-year-old woman with a deep cut to the palmar aspect of the hand at the base of the little finger cannot adduct or abduct the fingers. Which nerve is cut?
A) Median
B) Ulnar
C) Radial
D) Anterior interosseous
E) Posterior interosseous

Correct Answer: B
Explanation: The ulnar nerve supplies the interosseous muscles (palmar and dorsal), which abduct and adduct the fingers. Injury causes loss of finger abduction/adduction (positive Froment’s sign).

8. A 70-year-old man with benign prostatic hyperplasia has a Foley catheter inserted. Resistance is met at the membranous urethra. Which structure is the narrowest part of the male urethra?
A) Prostatic urethra
B) Membranous urethra
C) Bulbous urethra
D) Penile urethra
E) Bladder neck

Correct Answer: B
Explanation: The membranous urethra is the narrowest and least distensible part of the male urethra. It passes through the urogenital diaphragm and is a common site of traumatic stricture.

9. A 50-year-old woman with a right-sided breast mass undergoes a biopsy. The surgeon makes an incision in the upper outer quadrant. Which artery is most at risk for bleeding in this area?
A) Lateral thoracic artery
B) Internal thoracic artery
C) Thoracoacromial artery
D) Intercostal artery
E) Subclavian artery

Correct Answer: A
Explanation: The lateral thoracic artery runs along the lateral border of the pectoralis minor and supplies the upper outer quadrant of the breast. It is commonly encountered during breast surgery.

10. A 35-year-old man with a stab wound to the left anterior chest at the 5th intercostal space, midclavicular line, presents with distended neck veins and hypotension. Which condition is most likely?
A) Tension pneumothorax
B) Cardiac tamponade
C) Hemothorax
D) Pericarditis
E) Aortic dissection

Correct Answer: B
Explanation: Beck’s triad (hypotension, distended neck veins, muffled heart sounds) is classic for cardiac tamponade. The stab wound likely penetrated the right ventricle, causing hemopericardium.

11. A 60-year-old man with a history of smoking presents with hoarseness and a left lung mass. Which nerve is compressed by the mass as it loops under the aortic arch?
A) Left recurrent laryngeal
B) Right recurrent laryngeal
C) Left phrenic
D) Right phrenic
E) Left vagus

Correct Answer: A
Explanation: The left recurrent laryngeal nerve branches from the vagus and loops under the aortic arch. A left apical lung mass can compress it, causing hoarseness due to vocal cord paralysis.

12. A patient with cirrhosis develops caput medusae. Which vein recanalizes to cause this finding?
A) Paraumbilical vein
B) Left gastric vein
C) Splenic vein
D) Superior mesenteric vein
E) Inferior mesenteric vein

Correct Answer: A
Explanation: In portal hypertension, the paraumbilical veins recanalize and connect the left portal vein to the epigastric veins, causing dilated veins around the umbilicus (caput medusae).

13. A 25-year-old man with a fractured pelvis after a fall cannot flex his hip. Which nerve is most likely injured?
A) Femoral
B) Obturator
C) Sciatic
D) Superior gluteal
E) Inferior gluteal

Correct Answer: A
Explanation: The femoral nerve (L2–L4) supplies the iliopsoas (hip flexor). Pelvic fractures can injure this nerve, causing loss of hip flexion and difficulty climbing stairs.

14. A 55-year-old woman with a central venous catheter in the right subclavian vein develops a right-sided pneumothorax. The needle likely punctured which structure?
A) Lung apex
B) Subclavian artery
C) Brachiocephalic vein
D) Thoracic duct
E) Phrenic nerve

Correct Answer: A
Explanation: The lung apex (cupula) extends above the first rib into the root of the neck. A misplaced subclavian needle can puncture the apical pleura, causing pneumothorax.

15. A 30-year-old woman with a deep cut to the medial aspect of the thigh cannot adduct the thigh. Which nerve is injured?
A) Femoral
B) Obturator
C) Sciatic
D) Saphenous
E) Lateral femoral cutaneous

Correct Answer: B
Explanation: The obturator nerve (L2–L4) supplies the adductor muscles of the thigh. Injury from a medial thigh wound causes loss of hip adduction.

16. A patient with a large hiatal hernia presents with heartburn and regurgitation. Through which diaphragmatic hiatus does the hernia occur?
A) Aortic hiatus
B) Esophageal hiatus
C) Caval hiatus
D) Foramen of Morgagni
E) Foramen of Bochdalek

Correct Answer: B
Explanation: The esophageal hiatus (at T10) transmits the esophagus and vagal trunks. Hiatal hernias occur when the stomach herniates through this hiatus into the thorax.

17. A 65-year-old man with a history of alcohol abuse presents with severe epigastric pain radiating to the back. Which retroperitoneal organ is inflamed?
A) Stomach
B) Pancreas
C) Duodenum
D) Gallbladder
E) Left kidney

Correct Answer: B
Explanation: The pancreas is a retroperitoneal organ lying posterior to the stomach. Acute pancreatitis causes epigastric pain radiating to the back due to its retroperitoneal location.

18. A 40-year-old man with a stab wound to the right 7th intercostal space, midaxillary line, has a right-sided hemothorax. Which intercostal artery is injured?
A) 6th
B) 7th
C) 8th
D) 5th
E) 9th

Correct Answer: B
Explanation: The 7th intercostal artery runs in the subcostal groove of the 7th rib. A stab at the 7th ICS will injure the artery of that space, causing bleeding into the pleural cavity.

19. A 50-year-old woman with a thyroid nodule undergoes fine-needle aspiration. The needle passes through the platysma. What is the next fascial layer?
A) Superficial cervical fascia
B) Investing layer of deep cervical fascia
C) Pretracheal fascia
D) Prevertebral fascia
E) Carotid sheath

Correct Answer: B
Explanation: After skin and platysma (in superficial fascia), the next layer is the investing layer of deep cervical fascia, which encloses the sternocleidomastoid and trapezius muscles.

20. A 70-year-old man with a left femoral neck fracture undergoes hemiarthroplasty. During the posterior approach, which nerve is most at risk?
A) Sciatic
B) Femoral
C) Obturator
D) Superior gluteal
E) Inferior gluteal

Correct Answer: A
Explanation: The sciatic nerve lies posterior to the hip joint and is vulnerable during posterior hip approaches. Retractor placement can injure it, causing foot drop or leg weakness.

21. A patient with a central line in the left internal jugular vein develops a chylothorax. Which duct is lacerated?
A) Right lymphatic duct
B) Thoracic duct
C) Azygos vein
D) Hemiazygos vein
E) Intercostal lymphatic

Correct Answer: B
Explanation: The thoracic duct empties into the left subclavian vein. Puncture during left IJ cannulation can lacerate it, causing chylothorax (milky pleural effusion).

22. A 35-year-old man with a fractured humeral shaft cannot extend his wrist. Which nerve is injured?
A) Axillary
B) Radial
C) Median
D) Ulnar
E) Musculocutaneous

Correct Answer: B
Explanation: The radial nerve runs in the spiral groove of the humerus. Fracture of the humeral shaft often injures it, causing wrist drop (loss of wrist and finger extensors).

23. A 60-year-old woman with a large right-sided pleural effusion undergoes thoracentesis. The needle is inserted just above the 8th rib in the posterior axillary line. Which principle avoids the neurovascular bundle?
A) The bundle runs below the rib
B) The bundle runs above the rib
C) The bundle is midline
D) The bundle is extrapleural
E) The bundle is protected by endothoracic fascia

Correct Answer: A
Explanation: The intercostal neurovascular bundle runs in the subcostal groove of the upper rib (i.e., below each rib). Needle insertion just above the lower rib avoids the bundle of the rib below.

24. A 45-year-old man with a stab wound to the epigastrium has a CT showing a pancreatic injury. Which part of the pancreas is most anterior and commonly injured?
A) Head
B) Neck
C) Body
D) Tail
E) Uncinate process

Correct Answer: C
Explanation: The body of the pancreas is the most anterior and least protected part, lying directly posterior to the stomach. It is most commonly injured in penetrating epigastric trauma.

25. A 30-year-old woman with a deep cut to the volar forearm, 2 cm proximal to the wrist, has loss of thumb opposition. Which nerve is cut?
A) Median (recurrent branch)
B) Ulnar (deep branch)
C) Radial (superficial)
D) Median (palmar cutaneous)
E) Ulnar (dorsal cutaneous)

Correct Answer: A
Explanation: The recurrent (thenar) branch of the median nerve supplies the thenar muscles (opponens pollicis, abductor pollicis brevis). Injury at the wrist causes loss of thumb opposition.

26. A patient with chronic liver disease develops ascites. Paracentesis is performed at the midline below the umbilicus. Which structure is avoided by this approach?
A) Inferior epigastric artery
B) Rectus abdominis
C) Linea alba
D) Superior epigastric artery
E) Deep circumflex iliac artery

Correct Answer: A
Explanation: The inferior epigastric artery runs lateral to the rectus abdominis. Midline puncture (linea alba) is avascular and avoids the inferior epigastric vessels, making it safer for paracentesis.

27. A 55-year-old man with a left-sided inguinal hernia undergoes repair. The hernia sac is lateral to the inferior epigastric vessels. Which type of hernia is this?
A) Direct inguinal
B) Indirect inguinal
C) Femoral
D) Obturator
E) Spigelian

Correct Answer: B
Explanation: Indirect inguinal hernias pass through the deep inguinal ring, which is lateral to the inferior epigastric vessels. Direct hernias are medial to these vessels.

28. A 40-year-old woman with a stab wound to the right upper quadrant at the 6th intercostal space, anterior axillary line, has a liver laceration. Which liver segment is most likely injured?
A) Segment II
B) Segment III
C) Segment IV
D) Segment V
E) Segment VII

Correct Answer: D
Explanation: Segment V (right anterior inferior) lies just deep to the 6th–7th intercostal spaces anteriorly. A stab here commonly injures segment V or VI.

29. A 70-year-old man with a known abdominal aortic aneurysm presents with sudden-onset testicular pain. Which nerve pathway mediates this referred pain?
A) Ilioinguinal
B) Genitofemoral
C) Sympathetic fibers via spermatic plexus
D) Obturator
E) Pudendal

Correct Answer: C
Explanation: Visceral afferent fibers from the aorta travel with sympathetic nerves that follow the testicular vessels, causing referred pain to the testicle (a classic sign of leaking AAA).

30. A 25-year-old man with a stab wound to the left posterior triangle of the neck presents with a winged scapula. Which nerve is injured?
A) Long thoracic
B) Spinal accessory
C) Dorsal scapular
D) Thoracodorsal
E) Suprascapular

Correct Answer: A
Explanation: The long thoracic nerve runs on the serratus anterior. Injury causes medial winging of the scapula (the medial border lifts off the chest wall when pushing against a wall).

31. A patient with a large goiter undergoes thyroidectomy. Postoperatively, she has a weak, breathy voice. Which nerve is most likely injured?
A) Recurrent laryngeal
B) External branch of superior laryngeal
C) Internal branch of superior laryngeal
D) Hypoglossal
E) Phrenic

Correct Answer: A
Explanation: Unilateral recurrent laryngeal nerve injury causes ipsilateral vocal cord paralysis, resulting in hoarseness and a weak, breathy voice. This is a common complication of thyroid surgery.

32. A 60-year-old man with a right-sided stroke has difficulty swallowing. Which cranial nerve is most likely affected?
A) Trigeminal (CN V)
B) Facial (CN VII)
C) Glossopharyngeal (CN IX)
D) Vagus (CN X)
E) Hypoglossal (CN XII)

Correct Answer: D
Explanation: The vagus nerve (CN X) provides motor innervation to the pharyngeal constrictors and laryngeal muscles. Lesions cause dysphagia and dysphonia.

33. A 50-year-old woman with a central line in the right internal jugular vein develops a right-sided Horner’s syndrome. Which structure was injured?
A) Stellate ganglion
B) Vagus nerve
C) Phrenic nerve
D) Recurrent laryngeal nerve
E) Brachial plexus

Correct Answer: A
Explanation: The stellate ganglion (cervicothoracic sympathetic ganglion) lies on the longus colli muscle at C7. A deep IJ puncture can injure it, causing ipsilateral Horner’s syndrome (ptosis, miosis, anhidrosis).

34. A 35-year-old man with a deep cut to the palmar aspect of the hand at the base of the thumb has loss of sensation over the palmar aspect of the index finger. Which nerve is injured?
A) Median
B) Ulnar
C) Radial
D) Palmar cutaneous branch of median
E) Superficial radial

Correct Answer: A
Explanation: The median nerve provides sensation to the palmar aspect of the lateral 3½ fingers (including the index finger). Injury at the wrist causes sensory loss in that distribution.

35. A patient with a large left-sided pneumothorax has a chest tube inserted at the 5th intercostal space, anterior axillary line. Which muscle is first encountered after skin?
A) Pectoralis major
B) Serratus anterior

Explanation: At the anterior axillary line, the serratus anterior is the most superficial muscle overlying the ribs. The chest tube passes through it before reaching the intercostals.

Medium (Q36–70)
36. A 55-year-old man with a left lung mass presents with hoarseness and a left-sided Horner’s syndrome. Which anatomical structures are involved?
A) Left recurrent laryngeal nerve and stellate ganglion
B) Left phrenic nerve and vagus
C) Left recurrent laryngeal and phrenic
D) Stellate ganglion alone
E) Brachial plexus upper trunk

Correct Answer: A
Explanation: A Pancoast tumor can invade both the left recurrent laryngeal nerve (causing hoarseness) and the stellate ganglion (causing Horner’s). This combination indicates apical lung cancer.

37. A patient with a gunshot wound to the epigastrium has a CT showing injury to the celiac trunk. Which organs would be most immediately ischemic?
A) Liver, spleen, stomach
B) Kidneys, adrenals
C) Small intestine, cecum
D) Descending colon
E) Pancreas, duodenum

Correct Answer: A
Explanation: The celiac trunk supplies the foregut: liver (via hepatic artery), spleen (via splenic artery), and stomach (via left gastric artery). The pancreas and duodenum receive additional supply from SMA.

38. A 45-year-old woman undergoes a right hemicolectomy for colon cancer. The surgeon identifies the ureter crossing which artery at the pelvic brim to avoid injury?
A) Common iliac artery
B) External iliac artery
C) Internal iliac artery
D) Gonadal vessels
E) Ovarian artery

Correct Answer: B
Explanation: The ureter crosses the external iliac artery at the pelvic brim, medial to the gonadal vessels. This is a critical landmark to avoid ureteral injury during pelvic or colorectal surgery.

39. A 60-year-old man with a ruptured abdominal aortic aneurysm undergoes emergency repair. The surgeon cross-clamps the aorta below the renal arteries. Which collateral pathway maintains spinal cord perfusion?
A) Artery of Adamkiewicz
B) Internal thoracic to intercostal
C) Vertebral to posterior intercostal
D) Superior mesenteric to lumbar
E) Iliolumbar to anterior spinal

Correct Answer: A
Explanation: The artery of Adamkiewicz (great radicular artery) arises from a left posterior intercostal or lumbar artery (T9–L2) and supplies the lower spinal cord. It is a critical collateral after aortic clamping.

40. A patient with a pancreatic head tumor presents with painless jaundice and a palpable gallbladder (Courvoisier’s sign). Which structure is compressed?
A) Common bile duct
B) Main pancreatic duct
C) Portal vein
D) Superior mesenteric artery
E) Hepatic artery

Correct Answer: A
Explanation: The common bile duct passes through the head of the pancreas. A pancreatic head tumor compresses the CBD, causing obstructive jaundice. The gallbladder becomes palpable due to distention.

Explanation: Hesselbach’s triangle is bounded by the inferior epigastric vessels (laterally), rectus abdominis (medially), and inguinal ligament (inferiorly). Direct hernias occur within this triangle.
